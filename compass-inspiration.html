This file pulls layout ideas from Tony Robbins’ core coaching frameworks — RPM, Wheel of Life, 6 Human Needs, the Primary Question, the North Star vision, and the Year Compass.

Quick glossary of the frameworks

RPM (Rapid Planning Method)

Robbins’ flagship planning system. Every goal starts with a Result (what you want), a Purpose (why it matters), and a Massive Action Plan (the concrete steps). “No purpose, no power.”

Wheel of Life

Rate 8 life areas 1–10 to see which are thriving and which are starving. The shape of the wheel tells you where your energy is imbalanced. Classic self-assessment tool.

6 Human Needs

Certainty, Variety, Significance, Love / Connection, Growth, Contribution. Robbins argues every goal you set is unconsciously trying to meet one of these six. Knowing your top 2 reveals why some goals stall.

Primary Question

The question you unconsciously ask yourself most often shapes your life. “How can I make this fun?” lives a different life than “Why does this always happen to me?” Setting it explicitly is a power move.

Identity Ladder

“Who I am” → “What I do” → “What I have.” Robbins teaches you lead with identity — change the first, the rest follow. The compass should make this explicit.

North Star + Roles

One 10-year vision, then your 4–6 life roles (Founder, Partner, Friend, Athlete…), each with its own mini-vision and current quarter goal. Treats life like a portfolio.

Variant 2 — Wheel of Life

Variant 3 — Identity Ladder

“Change the identity and the rest follows.”

framework · Identity Ladder

Explicitly stack the three layers: who you are, what you do, what you want. Robbins teaches you lead with identity. The top row is the most important one — your goals should be derived from it, not the other way around. Variant 4 — 6 Human Needs Assessment

Every goal meets a need. Know which.

framework · 6 Human Needs

Six sliders. Rate how fulfilled each need is right now. Goals can be tagged against needs — so a goal that’s tagged “Growth” but you’re already at 9/10 for Growth is probably the wrong goal. Robbins’ core teaching: your top 2 needs drive 90% of your behaviour.

Certainty6 / 10
Safety, comfort, predictability. Too high = boredom. Too low = anxiety.
Variety / Uncertainty8 / 10
Surprise, novelty, change. Your highest need right now — shows in the multi-project sprawl.
Significance7 / 10
Feeling important, unique, needed. Watch for ego traps.
Love / Connection5 / 10
Currently low — flagged last review. Scheduled dinners with old friends.
Growth9 / 10
Learning, expanding, developing. Consistently your top score for 6 months.
Contribution4 / 10
Giving beyond yourself. Lowest right now — consider a goal that serves others.
Compass insight
Your top 2 needs are Growth and Variety. That explains why you start new projects easily but struggle to finish — finishing satisfies Certainty, not Growth. Consider framing Phase 2 polish as a new problem (“how do I design the best compass tab”) instead of a “finish” task.
Variant 5 — North Star + Roles

One 10-year vision. Four roles. Each with a mini-compass.

Variant 6 — Year Compass (Q1–Q4)

One year, four quadrants. Each quarter has a theme.

framework · Year Compass / Annual Review

Zoom out. Plan in seasons. Each quarter gets a theme (a 1-word mood), a single must-achieve, and a few supporting goals. Current quarter expands in place. Past quarters show their score. Closest to how the current Quarterly Goals section works — but with temporal context.

Variant 7 — Primary Question

The question you ask yourself the most becomes your life.

framework · Primary Question

A decorative section at the top of compass (or a small ribbon on home). Forces you to articulate the question you want to live inside of. Robbins tells a story of switching his own from “how do I get more love?” to “how do I give more?” — said it changed his life. Not a functional data surface; a ritual one.

Primary Question
“How can I ship one thing today that I would actually want to use?”

My recommendation

The compass tab works best as a layered page, not a single framework. My strongest combination: V5 (North Star + Roles) at the top of the page as the anchor, then V6 (Year Compass) in the middle to show quarterly execution, and V2 (Wheel of Life) at the bottom as a self-assessment ribbon. V1 (RPM) becomes the “detail view” when you click into a goal. V7 (Primary Question) could be a thin strip at the very top instead of the journal bar on home.

V3 (Identity Ladder) and V4 (Human Needs) are powerful but don’t belong on the main compass tab — they fit better as sub-pages or as a quarterly review flow. They’d overwhelm daily use.
