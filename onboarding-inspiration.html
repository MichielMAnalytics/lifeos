Section 01

Struggle / Use Case Framing

How to frame the "What do you struggle with?" page. Current: 5 cards with struggle-based titles. Research shows this is the strongest pattern for coaching products.

Key insight: Calm, Noom, and Fabulous all lead with "What brings you here?" or "What are you struggling with?" rather than listing features. Coaching products convert 2-3x better when they mirror the user's pain, not the product's capabilities.
Option 1A Calm + Headspace

Emotional Struggle Cards

Large cards with illustrations. Each card is a feeling: "My days feel chaotic," "I keep losing focus," "I never finish what I start." Tap to select multiple. No descriptions needed — the title IS the description.

Pros: Maximum emotional resonance. Fast to scan.
Cons: Fewer cards means some struggles aren't represented.
Option 1B Noom + Fabulous

Progressive Struggle Quiz

Instead of one page with 5 cards, ask 3 quick questions one at a time (Typeform-style): (1) "What's your #1 challenge?" (2) "When does it affect you most?" (3) "What have you tried before?" Each answer refines the experience.

Pros: Feels more personal. Creates investment (sunk cost).
Cons: More steps before payment. Some users may drop off.
Option 1C Notion + Monday.com

Outcome-First Cards

Flip the framing from struggle to desired outcome: "I want to plan better days," "I want to hit my goals," "I want to reflect more." Each card shows a mini preview of what that looks like in LifeOS (screenshot or animation).

Pros: Aspirational, forward-looking. Shows the product early.
Cons: Less emotional hook than struggle-based. Requires demo assets.
Option 1D Current LifeOS

Struggle Cards + Demo Preview

Keep the current 5 struggle cards but add a preview panel on the right (like the connect page chat mockup). When a user selects "My days feel chaotic," the right panel shows a day plan demo. Selecting "I need accountability" shows the Life Coach chat.

Pros: Best of both worlds: emotional hook + product preview.
Cons: Requires 5 demo assets (screenshots or short videos). More complex to build.
Section 02

Pre-Payment Credibility

Building enough trust and desire before the paywall. The goal: users should want the product before they see the price.

Key insight: Noom inserts stats and testimonials between quiz questions, not just on the paywall. Fabulous leads with "Born at Duke University" for academic credibility. The most effective pattern: show value, then ask for payment.
Option 2A Noom

Interstitial Stats

Between onboarding steps, insert brief stat screens: "87% of LifeOS users complete their MIT every day" or "Users report 3x more weekly wins after 30 days." 2-3 seconds each, auto-advance with a skip option.

Pros: Builds trust without feeling like a sales pitch.
Cons: Need real data (or at least plausible data for beta).
Option 2B Headspace

Experience Before Payment

Let users experience one core feature before the paywall. For LifeOS: let them create their first day plan or have a quick exchange with the Life Coach. The paywall appears after they've felt the value, not before.

Pros: Highest-quality leads (they've already used it).
Cons: Requires the product to work pre-payment. Infrastructure complexity.
Option 2C Blinkist + Calm

Outcome Projection

After the struggle quiz, show a personalized "results" page: "Based on what you told us, here's your plan." Show a mock day plan or goal timeline with the user's selected focus areas filled in. "Your Life Coach is ready to help you with [X, Y, Z]."

Pros: Creates strong desire ("I want to see more of this").
Cons: Must feel genuine, not manipulative.
Section 03

Personalization Questions

What to ask during the post-payment personalization flow. Current: 6 questions in a chat-bubble UI. Research says 3-5 is the sweet spot for productivity apps; coaching apps can go longer.

Key insight from Sunsama: Every question should have immediate functional impact. If a question doesn't change the experience, remove it. Notion asks only 3 questions. Headspace asks 3. Noom asks 77 — but the quiz IS the product.
Option 3A Notion + Headspace

Minimal: 3 Questions

(1) "What should I call you?" (2) "What are your top goals?" (3) "How should I talk to you?" (tone). Each answer directly shapes the Life Coach's behavior. Done in under 60 seconds.

Pros: Zero friction. Respects user's time. Each question matters.
Cons: Less data for personalization. May feel too quick.
Option 3B Current LifeOS

Balanced: 6 Questions

Name, goals, focus areas, communication tone, biggest challenge, accountability style. Chat-bubble UI with progress bar. Each answer saves to the profile and shapes the Life Coach. About 2-3 minutes.

Pros: Good balance of data and speed. Feels thorough but not heavy.
Cons: Some questions may feel redundant (goals vs focus areas).
Option 3C Noom + Fabulous

Deep: Quiz as Product

10-15 questions that teach the user about themselves while collecting data. Include micro-insights between questions: "Interesting — people who struggle with finishing things often benefit from smaller daily goals." The quiz itself feels valuable.

Pros: Creates investment. Users feel understood. More data.
Cons: Risk of drop-off. Must be genuinely insightful, not filler.
Option 3D Linear

Skip to Action

No quiz at all. After payment, drop users directly into the Life Coach with a greeting: "Hey [name]! Tell me about your week — what's on your mind?" Let the AI learn naturally through conversation rather than a structured quiz.

Pros: Most natural for a chat product. Zero friction.
Cons: First interactions may feel generic until the AI learns enough.
Section 04

Pricing & Paywall

How to present the pricing page. Current: "You're one step away" with pricing card + founders note.

Option 4A Blinkist

The Honest Paywall

Radically transparent: clear trial timeline, explicit "we'll remind you before charging" promise, no hidden terms. Blinkist reported 23% conversion increase and 55% fewer complaints after adopting this pattern.

Pros: Builds massive trust. Reduces churn and support tickets.
Cons: Requires confidence in the product (no fear of trial cancellations).
Option 4B Headspace

Minimal Paywall

Just the price and a CTA. No feature lists, no comparisons, no long copy. The assumption: if the onboarding did its job, the user already wants it. Headspace shows one meditation BEFORE the paywall, then just "Continue for $12.99/mo."

Pros: Clean, confident, premium feel.
Cons: Only works if pre-paywall experience is strong enough.
Option 4C Noom

Personalized Plan Summary

Before showing the price, display a "Your personalized plan" summary based on quiz answers. "Based on your goals, we recommend [X]. Your Life Coach will focus on [Y, Z]." Then show the price as an afterthought. The price feels justified because the plan feels custom.

Pros: Price feels earned and personalized. High conversion.
Cons: Can feel manipulative if the "personalization" is fake.
Section 05

Post-Payment Setup

The setup flow after payment: Skip / Quick Connect / Full Setup. Research shows setup should produce real output, not just configure settings.

Option 5A Motion + Linear

Setup Produces Real Output

Instead of configuring settings, the setup creates something real: your first day plan, your first goal, or your first journal entry. Linear teaches features through a checklist of real tasks. Motion builds a real project template during setup.

Pros: User has something valuable immediately. Setup = first use.
Cons: More complex to build. Harder to skip.
Option 5B Sunsama

Drip Onboarding (1 feature/day)

Don't reveal everything at once. Day 1: set up your day plan. Day 2: the Life Coach introduces goal tracking. Day 3: learn about journaling. Each day, the Life Coach sends a message introducing one new capability. By day 10, users have organically adopted the full product.

Pros: No overwhelm. Each feature feels like a personal recommendation.
Cons: Slower time-to-value for power users who want everything now.
Option 5C Monday.com

Pre-Populated Dashboard

Show the dashboard pre-filled with example data based on the user's struggle selections. "My days feel chaotic" → show a beautiful day plan with sample tasks. User sees their future self in the product, then clears the sample data and starts fresh.

Pros: Instant "aha moment." User sees the product's potential immediately.
Cons: Sample data can feel fake. Need good placeholder content.
Section 06

Demo & Preview Patterns

How to show demos during the use case / struggle selection page. These assets need to be recorded by Kemp.

What to record: 5 short demos (10-15 seconds each), one per struggle card. Can be screen recordings, GIFs, or short Loom-style videos. Show the actual LifeOS experience for each use case.
Option 6A Monday.com

Hover Preview

When user hovers over a struggle card, a looping GIF or video plays inside the card showing that feature in action. No click needed. Fast, engaging, zero-friction.

Option 6B Notion

Side Panel Preview

Struggle cards on the left, live preview panel on the right (like current connect page). Selecting a card swaps the preview. Desktop only — on mobile, show preview below the cards.

Option 6C Linear

Background Video

A single looping video of the LifeOS dashboard plays behind the struggle cards (slightly blurred/dimmed). Gives an impression of the product without needing per-card demos. Simple to produce, high impact.

Section 07

Chat-Based Onboarding

Making the personalization feel like a conversation with the Life Coach (current approach). How to make it feel more alive.

Option 7A Current LifeOS

Static Chat Bubbles

Pre-scripted questions in chat-bubble UI. Structured inputs (chips, cards) for answers. Fast, predictable, clean. Current implementation with progress bar.

Pros: Fast, no API calls, works offline. Consistent experience.
Cons: Feels scripted. Not truly conversational.
Option 7B Future LifeOS

Hybrid: Static Start, Live Finish

Start with 3-4 structured questions (static). After the last answer, hand off to the real Life Coach with all context: "Great, I've got everything I need. Is there anything else you'd like me to know?" The transition from scripted to live feels magical.

Pros: Best of both worlds. Users experience the real product at the end.
Cons: Requires deployment to be ready. Timing dependency.
Option 7C Noom

Quiz with Micro-Insights

Between questions, the "coach" shares a relevant insight: "Fun fact: people who set their MIT the night before are 2x more likely to complete it." Makes the quiz educational, not just data collection.

Pros: Quiz feels valuable in itself. Users learn while answering.
Cons: Longer flow. Insights must be genuinely useful.
Section 08

Hints & Progressive Nudges

Post-onboarding hints in the bottom-right of the app. Current: 5 condition-based hints with 7-day cooldown and 1-hour gap.

Option 8A Monday.com

Action-Card Checklist

Instead of individual hints, show a persistent checklist card: "Get started: [ ] Set your first goal [ ] Create a day plan [ ] Connect Telegram." Progress bar fills as items are completed. Dismissable.

Option 8B Sunsama

Life Coach Drip Messages

No separate hint UI. Instead, the Life Coach sends proactive messages: "Hey! I noticed you haven't set up Telegram yet. Want me to walk you through it?" More natural, uses existing UI (chat), and feels personal.

Option 8C Current LifeOS

Subtle Bottom-Right Cards

Current implementation: small cards with dismiss, 7-day cooldown, 1-hour gap. Non-intrusive, condition-based. Simple and clean.

Section 09

Social Proof Patterns

When and how to show social proof. Research: show it AFTER the user forms their own opinion, not during initial exploration.

Option 9A Noom

Precise Numbers + Testimonials

"47,392 people started their free trial this week." Precise numbers feel more authentic than rounded ones. Pair with 1-2 short testimonials about outcomes, not features.

Option 9B Fabulous

Authority / Academic Credibility

"Built with behavioral science from [university/research]." For LifeOS: "Designed around the RPM method and MIT framework." Position the methodology, not the user count.

Option 9C Current LifeOS

Founders Note

Personal message from Kemp & Michiel with photos. "We built this for ourselves first." Authenticity over scale. Works especially well for early-stage products where user counts are small.

Section 10

Commitment Devices

Behavioral science techniques to increase follow-through after onboarding.

Research from Fabulous: Users who sign a "commitment contract" during onboarding are 20% more likely to follow through on their plan. Implementation intentions ("I will [behavior] at [time] in [location]") are the most effective format.
Option 10A Fabulous

Commitment Contract

After personalization, ask: "I commit to checking in with my Life Coach every [morning/evening] for the next 7 days." User taps to "sign." Creates psychological ownership and follow-through.

Option 10B Noom

Outcome Timeline

Show a projected timeline: "If you follow your plan, here's where you could be in 30 days." For LifeOS: "30 days of daily planning = [X] tasks completed, [Y] journal entries, [Z] goals tracked." Makes the future tangible.

Option 10C Rise

First Action Now

End onboarding with one immediate action: "Let's set your MIT for tomorrow. What's the one thing you need to do?" User types it. Done — they've already used the product. First action = first habit loop.
